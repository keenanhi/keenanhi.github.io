Frequently Asked Questions

What is mastering?


To many, mastering is simply adding volume to a track. Not to us! Our engineers understand mastering to be a truly vital "second opinion" of an audio work. In addition to adding volume and making sure your tracks are ready for release, our engineers take pride in enhancing the sonic quality of a work while retaining its original vision and fundamental spirit. Plus, we offer revisions to make sure you are fully satisfied with your master, and masters for our subscribers do roll over month to month. What is mix feedback?


Of course, creating the perfect track doesn't start at the mastering level; some mix decisions can make or break a great song. We know our customers want to learn how to mix like the pros, and that is why our mastering engineers offer mix feedback. It is our strong held belief that there is nothing more vital to growth than receiving feedback on your work from experienced ears. In fact, we believe this to be such a vital step to personal growth that we hold weekly feedback sessions in-house for our own engineers to critique the masters we work on (and of course our own musical projects!)

What is stem mastering?


Just plain unhappy with how your mix sounds? Stem mastering is the closest you can get to actually giving your mastering engineer the reins to your project. With stem mastering, you send us separate groups of tracks, bounced together on one audio file (called a stem). We then mix those stems, and master the result. For example, if you recorded a full drum kit, three guitars, and three vocal tracks, you could send us one stem with all the drum tracks, one stem with all the guitar tracks, and one stem with all the vocal tracks, each mixed to your liking. For those times when you are banging your ears against a wall, stem mastering is a perfect solution.
